Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Your Master Password cannot be recovered or reset. Due to our Zero-Knowledge security architecture, your Master Password is your one and only key. We never store it, so we cannot recover it for you. If you lose it, your encrypted data will be inaccessible forever. Please store your Master Password in a very safe place.

Yes, it's very safe. All of your vault data (site names, usernames, passwords, notes) is encrypted on your device using a key derived from your Master Password before it is sent to our servers. The data stored on our server is just a meaningless encrypted blob. We, not even our system administrators, can see the contents of your vault.

You can change your Master Password from the Profile page. Please note that changing your Master Password involves re-encrypting your entire vault with a new key. For security reasons, you will be automatically logged out from all devices after a successful Master Password change.

The Security Score is a general health indicator for your vault. It is calculated based on several factors, including the percentage of strong passwords, the number of weak passwords, and the number of reused passwords. A higher score means better overall password hygiene.

Currently, our application requires an active internet connection to log in and synchronize your encrypted vault from the server. Offline access is not yet supported in this version.

If you suspect unauthorized access, first try to change your Master Password on the Profile page. If you cannot log in or want to immediately delete all your vault data for security reasons, you can use our emergency feature.
